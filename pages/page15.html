The man said, "Look! The white Fangs!" The other indians laughed loudly. The grey cub felt two strong instincts - to sit still and to fight. So he bit the man's hand without hesitation. The man was angry. He hit the grey cub on the head angrily. Then there was a loud noise. The grey cub knew what it was. His mother was snarling wildly as she ran. The cub cried gladly and waited for his mother impatiently. The man-animals went back slowly in return. Then one of the men looked at the she- wolf attentively. "Kitche!" cried the man. At that moment the cub looked at his angry mother. She clung to the ground and wagged her tail excitedly. The cub could not understand this. He was shocked. She, too, was afraid of the man-animals. The man came up to her and put his hand on her head. She did not bite him. Now she did not look angry at all. "It is not strange," said Grey Beaver. "Her father was a wolf and her mother was a dog. Once my brother tied her in the forest and she ran away. It's been a year since she ran away. It was the time of the hunger, and there was no meat for the dogs. She lived with the wolves." "You are right, Grey Beaver," said Three Eagles. "It is clear that his mother is Kitche," said Grey Beaver. "But his father was a wolf. His Fangs are white. So his name is White Fang. He is my dog. Kitche was my brother's dog. And my brother is dead now." Thus the cub received his name. The man-animals talked for a while.
