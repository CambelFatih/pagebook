Chapter 5

The outcast

Lip-Lip continued to attack White Fang without mercy. White Fang became angrier and more ferocious than ever before. wildness was a part of his everyday life. He received a reputation for wildness among the man-animals themselves. He was at the center of all the trouble in the camp. He was an outcast. All the dogs followed Lip-Lip as his pack. There was a difference between White Fang and them. Perhaps, they felt his wild-wolf nature and instinctively fought against him as often as possible. But White Fang could protect himself very well. He could fight against two dogs at the same time. In these fights, he learned two important things: how to protect himself in an unequal battle and how to seriously wound the dogs in a short time. He stood steadily on his feet during every fight and never fell on the ground in defeat. White Fang tried to fight quickly and then run away. So he learned to attack without warning. He ran quickly and bit wildly before his enemy could prepare to meet him. Thus he learned how to take a dog by surprise and win a fight. Every man and dog in the camp hated White Fang. He never felt safe. The teeth of every dog were set against him. The man-animals met him with stones and curses. This is why he was always attentive and careful. The camp dogs ran as a pack all the time because they were afraid to meet White Fang alone. Lip-Lip was the only dog in the camp who could face White Fang and fight with him equally.
