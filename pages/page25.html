The rule he learned was to be strong and kill the weak. Grey Beaver was a strong god. That is why White Fang obeyed him. His development was very quick. He was faster than the other dogs, more intelligent and cunning. In the fall of that year, White Fang got his chance to take his freedom back. There was a big commotion in the camp for a few days. The tribe was preparing to go off for fall hunting. A trip to get meat that must last them all winter. White Fang watched it all very attentively. The canoes were floating away down the river, one by one. At that moment White Fang decided to run away into the forest. He ran as quickly as he could. Then he hid himself in a thick bush and waited. Time passed by, and he slept for hours. Later on, he heard Grey Beaver's voice. White Fang shook with fear, but he did not come out of the bush. The night came very soon. White Fang began to feel very lonely in the forest. He lay still and listened to the silence of the forest. Nothing moved. Then it was cold. There was no warm fire next to him. A short time later, he was very hungry. His bondage had made him soft and scared now that he was alone in nature. He had forgotten how to live in the forest. He was starting to panic, so he ran quickly toward the camp. But there was no camp near the river anymore. The camp had gone away. White Fang was very scared. There was no place to run. He went to where Grey Beaver's tepee had once stood. He smelled the ground and then sat down. He looked at the moon sadly. The next moment he howled loudly. With his howl he released his fear, loneliness, and all the sorrows of his long howl - the first howl he had ever made.
