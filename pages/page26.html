After that he lay on the ground and fell asleep. In the morning, he began to run along the river bank down the stream. He ran all day long. He neither stopped nor rested. His body seemed to ignore tiredness and hunger. He was following the trail of the gods without any hesitation. He ran all night, too. But by the afternoon of the second day, he had to stop because of his extreme tiredness. He was weak with hunger. His feet were bleeding and his fur was very dirty. The night came quickly. The snow was flying in the air. White Fang walked slowly along the river bank. Suddenly, he found a fresh trail in the snow. He knew what it was. He began to run along the river bank among the trees quickly. Soon he heard the sounds of the camp. Then he saw the light of the camp fire and Grey Beaver eating his meat by the fire. There was meat at the camp! He went up to the fire carefully. He knew that Grey Beaver could beat him terribly at any moment. Grey Beaver saw him and stopped eating his meat. White Fang lay at his master's feet silently. He shook with fear and waited for the punishment of his master. But the expected punishment did not come. Instead, Grey Beaver threw a piece of meat to White Fang. White Fang was very surprised. He ate his meat right away. After that he looked at the fire that warmed him. He had returned to the camp of the man-animals again. It was his own decision. He depended on the gods...
