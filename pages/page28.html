In December Grey Beaver went on a journey up the Mackenzie River. His sons, Mit-sah and Kloo-Kooch, went with him. They drove a big sled, which a team of dogs pulled hard and quickly through the snow. The sled was very useful, as it carried about two hundred pounds of food. White Fang had seen the camp dogs in the harness before. That is why he did not run away when Grey Beaver put him into the harness. There was a thin belt around his chest and over his back. Then his master fastened him to the sled with a long rope. There were seven dogs in the team. The ropes were different sizes to prevent the dogs from attacking each other during a long journey. The dog behind could never bite the one in the front. Grey Beaver knew how much Lip-Lip hated White Fang. Thus he put Lip-lip at the front of the longest rope. Lip-Lip became the leader of the pack. It was a great honour, no doubt. But, in fact, all the dogs in the pack began to hate him because of this honour that he was given. They tried to outrun him in the harness and bite him all day long. White Fang pulled the sled very well. White Fang worked hard, learned discipline, and was attentive. faithfulness and readiness characterised his work. He was much stronger physically than the other dogs in the harness. The dogs did not try to attack him the whole time. Now their anger and hatred were focused on Lip-Lip. He always stayed away from them anyway. Lip-Lip was the leader in the harness, but he had lost his leadership in the pack. All the dogs became his enemies once and for all.
