Mit-sah or Kloo-kooch. Anyway, White Fang always stayed away from the dogs. There was no friendship between White Fang and the other dogs. He never wanted to play with the other dogs. He ignored them completely. So they left him alone in his isolation. They got out of his way when he came along. They never tried to steal his food, out of fear that he would attack them. White Fang knew only how to fight, and he fought with them very skilfully. He knew the rule perfectly: to kill the weak and obey the strong. Thus White Fang was always alone. He fought often to keep his isolation. Such fights were short. He was too quick and clever for the others. He made other dogs respect him completely. He became a tyrant. He killed the weak, but he respected the strong. And the man-animals were strong to him. The months passed by. The journey of Grey Beaver still continued. White Fang became more powerful during the long hours on the trail. He had learned very well the world in which he lived. His world was hard and dangerous. It was a world without kindness, love, or the bright sweetness of the spirit. He did not love Grey Beaver. Grey Beaver was his master and god. But White Fang obeyed him only because of his power and lordship. Grey Beaver never said a kind word to him or stroked him kindly. It was not his way. He could only show his power with a club, and nothing else. So White Fang knew nothing of the kind feelings of men. moreover, he did not like the hands of the man-animals. He was afraid of them. Sometimes they gave him meat, but more often they hurt him. White Fang always kept away from the hands of the man-animals. When somebody tried to touch him, he got up. He did not like it.
