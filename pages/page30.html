White Fang saw the fight first. One of his gods was in trouble for sure. A crazy impulse made White Fang do what he did then. He jumped on the boys who had attacked Mit-sah. Five minutes later, the boys were running away as fast as they could. White Fang showed them that his teeth were sharp and dangerous. Soon Mit-sah told his father the whole story. Grey Beaver gave a big piece of meat to White Fang and said that he had done well. He ate his big piece of meat by the fire gladly. So he understood another rule: he must protect the gods who are close to his master. The months went by. The connection between dog and god grew stronger and stronger. This connection was very old. White Fang understood this connection very well. The terms were simple. A wolf exchanged his freedom for food. He received food, fire, and protection from the god. In return, a wolf had to protect his master, his family, and house. His loyalty to man was the most important law in his life. White Fang did his duties faithfully. But he did his duties because of fear, not of love. He did not know what love was. He never felt it. Kitche was a far off memory. He did not remember his mother's love at all.
