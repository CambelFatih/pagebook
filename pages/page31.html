Chapter 7

The Hunger

came quickly. Grey Beaver finished his long The spring journey. It was April. White Fang was already a two-year-old dog. He was the largest dog in the camp after Lip-Lip. His fur was grey. He looked like a real wolf. The blood of dog had left no mark on him physically. He walked through the camp with great confidence. He saw many dogs and puppies in the camp. White Fang was not afraid of them anymore. He had a lot of faith and pride in himself. He walked seriously among the grown dogs. His attitude toward them was less respectful. Nevertheless, he wanted neither trouble nor battle. His pride needed attention. He knew his rights in the camp very well. The other dogs had to respect him, that was all. There was no place for ignorance. So they accepted him as an equal dog among them. All White Fang wanted was loneliness. The dogs understood it very well after many wild fights. As a result, they quickly learned to leave him alone, and thus he left them unhurt as well. One sunny day, something unexpected had happened. White Fang was walking along the camp to search for a new tepee at the edge of the camp. Suddenly, he met Kitche. He stopped and looked at her. He remembered her very well. His memory became clear again. She had been the center of the universe to him. The old feelings of his past came back to him. He tried to go closer to her at once. But she snarled at him angrily.
