White Fang tried to smell the puppies, his brothers and sisters. But Kitche attacked him for the second time. White Fang went away looking at his mother stupidly. There was no place for him in her life. She had forgotten him and he had to forget her, too. All the old memories died again, but this time forever. That day, White Fang howled sadly for a long time. He was afraid of death and the unknown greatly. The months went by. White Fang grew stronger, heavier, and angrier. His character was developing according to his environment. Thus his environment made him tough and strong. He had a perfect shape physically. But he was becoming sadder and lonelier. All the dogs in the camp were afraid of him. They learned that it was better to be at peace with him than at war. That is why Grey Beaver began to respect him more day by day. Nevertheless, he had one weakness. He hated moments when somebody laughed loudly at him. The laughter of men was a terrible thing. They could laugh at anything they wanted for at except him. Laughter made him act like a demon. Sometimes Grey Beaver laughed at him just for fun. At such moments White Fang was beyond himself with anger. Anyway, he knew the law very well. He never bit his god with a club. He endured his anger as much as he could. In the third year of his life, there was another great hunger for the Mackenzie indians. In the summer, there were no fish in the river. In the winter, the moose and the other animals left the forest. In this time of hunger only the strong survived. The women and children cried bitterly in the camp. The men had nothing to hunt in the forest. The dogs ate one another.
