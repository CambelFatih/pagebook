Chapter 8

The Enemy

Grey Beaver decided to put White Fang at the front of the longest rope in the harness. That made him the leader of the sled team. At that time the dogs hated him very much. And White Fang hated them bitterly, too. He had to pull the sled or die on the trail. His spirit and will to live was too strong to let him give up. So he pulled the sled quickly all day long, avoiding the teeth of the angry dogs behind him. In these hard conditions, his anger and hatred grew stronger and stronger. He had no mercy. The pack tried to attack him all the time, and he left his teeth marks on the pack in return. White Fang did not stay near the gods for protection. He walked bravely about the camp, attacking the dogs who had bitten him during the day. Thus the pack learned to get out of his way and leave him alone. But the next day the dogs seemed to forget their lesson and continued to attack White Fang over and over again. White Fang was a perfect sled leader. He learned quickly. It was in his nature to learn quickly to survive in the hard conditions. His fighting skills continued to develop as well. Every day he fought with the pack in an unequal battle. To them White Fang was something unknown, terrible, and dangerous. He was a wild danger and horror. So when they fought with him they were protecting themselves against the death that shone in the shadows of the forest. But there was one lesson the dogs had learned very well- to keep together.
