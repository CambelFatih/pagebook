When White Fang was five years old, Grey Beaver took him on another long journey. They visited many villages along the Mackenzie River. They travelled across the Rockies and down to Porcupine and Yukon. White Fang met lots of strange dogs in those places. But they had no chance against him. He avoided their teeth by twisting out of the way just in time. They could not even touch White Fang because of his quick movements. His instincts told him the right time to attack, and when to back off. His eyes saw perfectly and his body moved so fast. In summer they arrived at fort Yukon. There stood the old Hudson's Bay Company fort. There were many indians, too. It was the summer of 1898, and thousands of gold-hunters were going to the Yukon and the Klondike. Here Grey Beaver ended his long journey. He had lots of furs and moccasins in his sled to sell at a good price. He wanted to earn good money, and like a true indian he began to live and sell his goods at fort Yukon. At fort Yukon, White Fang saw his first white man. White men were to him another kind of gods. They seemed more powerful and superior. White Fang was surprised by their intelligence. Grey Beaver looked less powerful than the white gods. White Fang never tried to go closer to the white gods. He always kept his distance. The white gods could not touch him. White Fang always showed them his sharp teeth and angry eyes.
