Grey Beaver did not sell the dog. He had become rich and did not need anything. Besides, White Fang was a special dog, the strongest dog he had ever had in his life. There was no dog like him from the Mackenzie River to Yukon. No, White Fang was not for sale at any price. But Beauty Smith knew the indians very well. He often visited Grey Beaver's tepee with a bottle of whisky under his coat. Soon Grey Beaver wanted more and more of the whisky. He was ready to do anything to have that desired bottle of whisky. He spent all his money on whisky. He was angry and poor. At that time Beauty Smith decided to talk with him again about the sale of White Fang. But this time he offered his price in bottles, not dollars. Thus Grey Beaver agreed to sell White Fang to Beauty Smith. That evening, White Fang lay down by Grey Beaver's tepee. The bad man had left at last. Shortly, Grey Beaver came up to him and tied a long rope around his neck. Then he sat down by White Fang, holding the end of the long rope in his hand. In the other hand he held a bottle. An hour passed by. White Fang heard somebody coming up to the tepee. He recognized this man right away. It was Beauty Smith. White Fang tried to grab the rope out of his master's hand. But Grey Beaver held the rope very tightly. Beauty Smith stopped and looked over White Fang carefully. He snarled at him angrily. Then he showed his teeth like a snake. At that moment Grey Beaver hit White Fang on his head and told him to sit quietly. After that he gave the end of the rope to Beauty Smith. The white man started to walk away pulling the rope behind him. White Fang resisted it. Grey Beaver pushed him away with a strong blow. He obeyed stupidly. He could not understand what was going on at that moment. White Fang followed the stranger looking back at Grey Beaver sadly.
