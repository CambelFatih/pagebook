White Fang was a true winner. Then there was a payment of bets and many men handed money to Beauty Smith. White Fang looked at the men around his pen attentively. It meant a fight, and it was the only way of expressing his miserable life. Beauty Smith kept him as a prisoner. So White Fang could satisfy his hatred only during a fight with a dog. From this day on, Beauty Smith began to bring many dogs into the pen where White Fang had lived so lonelily. And all of them White Fang killed one by one, easily and gladly. In the fall of that year, Beauty Smith began a long journey with White Fang on a ship to Dowson. At that time White Fang already had a reputation for being the fiercest dog in the land. His new name was "the fighting Wolf." His fame traveled far and wide. Curious men always surrounded his cage. He snarled at them or lay silently in the cage. Why did he hate them? He never asked himself that question. He knew only hate and lost himself in it completely. Life became hell for him. Nevertheless, nature had made him very tough. His spirit never broke. He adjusted himself to a miserable life and lived. People treated him disrespectfully by laughing at him and poking sticks into the cage to make him snarl. White Fang's hatred grew stronger and stronger with every passing day. Beauty Smith had a devil in him, and White Fang had another. And the two of them fought with each other nonstop. White Fang once submitted to a man with a club in his hand. This wisdom never left him. Anyway, he could never submit to Beauty Smith fully. White Fang could always bite him, no matter how terrible and angry his master was.
