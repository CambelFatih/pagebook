When the ship arrived at Dowson, White Fang went ashore. He was still at the center of public attention. Many curious men looked at him with interest. They paid fifty cents to see "the fighting Wolf." Beauty Smith kept White Fang angry all the time to earn as much money as possible. He gave his wolf no rest. He woke White Fang up with a stick so that the audience might see the wolf in full anger. His dog was a professional fighting animal. He was known as the most terrible of wild animals. That is why he arranged many fights for White Fang every day. White Fang fought with all kinds of dogs. It was a wild land, the men had no mercy, and the fights were usually to the death. White Fang was the winner every time. He never knew defeat. He was an experienced fighter, with his own tricks and methods. There was the power with which he clung to life. No dog could make him fall down. No dog could move as quickly as he. All dogs fought with him, and all failed. Men told this to one another and waited for White Fang's victory. And White Fang always pleased them. Soon there were no animals with which White Fang could fight with as equals. But after some time, a man came to the Klondike with the first bull-dog that had ever entered the Klondike. This dog and White Fang would fight inevitably. Everybody in the town waited for the coming fight with joy.
