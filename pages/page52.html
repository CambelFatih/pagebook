His love was silent and deep. He could show his love only by growling softly and through his shining eyes. He watched his god's every moment. White Fang began to like Matt as well. But he did not love him. He was just a friend of his master. So White Fang got used to his new life and master. He pulled his master's sled with the other dogs every day. He worked very faithfully. He was a leader in the harness, the cleverest and strongest among the other dogs. White Fang worked in the day and protected his master's house at night. He was truly attentive all the time a loyal dog to his loving master. In spring White Fang was in deep trouble, though. His beloved master disappeared without warning. That day, his master packed his bags and went somewhere far away. White Fang waited for his master near the door all that night. But his master did not return. In the morning, the door opened and Matt stepped inside. White Fang looked at him attentively, but he could not ask him what he wanted to know. The days came and went, and still his master did not return. White Fang became very sick. One day, Matt wrote a letter to Weedon Scott: "That crazy wolf neither works nor eats. He's very sick and sad now. I keep him in the house. He wants to see you very much. I don't know what to do with him. Maybe he'll die." That was true. White Fang was very weak and sad. He lost his heart. In the house he lay near the stove without any interest in life or food. And so a few days went by. One night, White Fang heard a loud cry.
