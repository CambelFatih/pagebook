Then came the day when White Fang saw a large bag on the floor and his beloved master putting his things into it. Besides, the atmosphere in the house was sad and silent. It was clear proof that something was wrong. White Fang had already felt preparing for it. He understood it very well now. His god was another long journey. moreover, White Fang was sure that his master would not take him with him this time. That night, he began to howl sadly and loudly. He pointed his nose at the cold stars and told them of his great sadness. At that moment the two men had gone to bed. They did their best to sleep normally. The next day White Fang's sadness and fear became even more terrible. He followed his master and guarded the door everytime he was inside. Through the open door he could still see the bag on the floor. Shortly, two indians arrived. White Fang watched them attentively as they lifted his master's bag onto the carriage and went away quickly. But White Fang did not follow them. The master was still in the house. Soon the master came to the door and called White Fang inside. "You poor devil," said Weedon Scott, "I'm going to make a long journey again. You cannot follow me, White Fang. Now you can growl at me kindly for the last time." But White Fang refused to growl. Instead, he put his head between the master's hand and body. "The ship has arrived," said Matt. "Say good-bye to the wolf and close the door. Hurry up!" The two men closed the door quickly. From inside the door came a loud cry. Then there was a long howl. White Fang was crying with pain and sadness. He tried to open the door, but failed again and again. The two men went to the ship sadly. The Aurora was the first steam-ship heading for America that year.
