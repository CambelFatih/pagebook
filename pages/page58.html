White Fang got off the ship when they landed in San Francisco. He was shocked. The powers of the gods seemed so great and limitless to him. There were many high buildings made of stone and glass. The streets were full of different vehicles: wagons and carriages drawn by horses, and even cars. All this was a demonstration of their power. The men controlled everything in the city through their power. White Fang felt very scared. Fear did not leave his heart for a minute. He felt very small and helpless. And there were so many gods! The crowd and the noise of the streets made his fear grow even bigger and stronger. White Fang was surprised at the endless movement of things. That is why he tried to walk close to his master the whole time. The city was a nightmare to White Fang, unreal and terrible. Soon his master put him onto a tram and tied him in a corner. He stood next to him, stroking him with his hand kindly. Later on, White Fang and his master got off of the tram. He was surprised again. The terrible city still did not end. It seemed to be absolutely endless. Anyway, the noise of the city was not as loud as before. Before him he saw a sunny and kind country. A carriage waited for them on the street. A man and a woman came up to his master joyfully. Then the woman's hands hugged his master kindly and tightly. Such a sudden act! White Fang snarled right away. "It's all right, mother," said Scott. "He doesn't understand that you're my mother. He'll learn very soon."
