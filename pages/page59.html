"So I can hug my son when his dog is not around then," she said. After that Weedon Scott spoke softly to White Fang until he had become quiet. Then the man took the master's bag into the carriage. The love-master went to the carriage with the woman, and White Fang followed them carefully. The horse began to run quickly. After fifteen minutes, the carriage stopped near a stone gateway. There were many trees and fields around a stone house. The place was huge, it seemed to be the biggest house he had ever seen. But White Fang had no time to see it all. White Fang walked to a big garden, following his master closely. Suddenly, a big dog attacked him from behind. White Fang turned back to attack his enemy without warning as usual. But he stopped at once. It was a female. The law of the wolves did not allow him to attack any female. So he stood still in shock, looking at the female dog attentively. But the dog still wanted to attack him. She was afraid of wolves, and White Fang was a real wolf to her. She snarled at him angrily for some time. "Collie!" cried a man in the garden. Weedon Scott laughed. "Never mind, father," said Scott. "It's a good lesson for White Fang. He'll learn many things here." The carriage went away. Collie still tried to bite White Fang with her sharp teeth. The situation was desperate. White Fang tried to run away from her, but she followed him quickly. Luckily, he ran up to his master and looked at him pleadingly. Soon the female dog also ran up to Weedon Scott. She was full of anger and hatred against wolves from the wild. She struck White Fang on his shoulder without warning.
