Chapter 16

The God's House

The name of Weedon Scott's house was Sierra Vista. White Fang could get used to life there, and enjoyed exploring the new places and things. So he quickly began to make himself at home. He had no serious trouble with the dogs, except for Collie. The other dogs understood at once that their master had allowed White Fang to stay inside the house. That is why they never tried to attack White Fang in Sierra Vista. White Fang did not want to fight with the dogs, either. He just wanted to be alone. His whole life he was alone, and he still wanted to be alone. But with Collie the situation was different. She never left him in peace. She did her best to make his life as hard as possible. So Collie bit him every day without mercy. His instinct held him back from attacking her, but he could not ignore her easily. When she struck him painfully, he ran away without trying to attack her in return. White Fang ignored her and avoided her as much as possible. So when he saw or heard her, he got up and walked out of her way. There were many things for White Fang to learn. Life in the Southland seemed harder than in the Northland. First, he had to learn about the family of the master. Actually, he was ready to do this. But there were too many people to remember. There was Judge Scott, and there was his wife. There were the master's two sisters, Beth and Merry. There was his wife, Alice. And there were his children, Weedon Jr. and Maude. Nobody I could tell him about all these people, of course. Anyway, White Fang quickly learned the master's family. He was kind to them because these people were important to his master.
