White Fang allowed all the members of the family to stroke him, but he never growled softly like he did with Weedon. He could give his growl only to his master, and nobody else could make him sound that way. Also, White Fang quickly learned the difference between the family and the servants of the house. All the servants of the house were afraid of him. There was a peace between them and White Fang, but nothing more. They worked about the house just as Matt had done in Klondike. In short, they were just servants to him. The master's voice and hands were the strongest things to White Fang. The master's voice was always enough for White Fang. Just by hearing the master's voice White Fang knew whether he did right or wrong. This voice allowed him to learn a new land and life. In the Northland, the only tamed animal was the dog. All other animals lived in the wild. All his life White Fang killed living things for food. But in the Southland it was different. This difference he had to learn in Sierra Vista, too. One morning, White Fang went up to a chicken, caught it, and ate it quickly. White Fang's natural instinct was to eat it. That day he saw one more chicken in Sierra Vista. He ran up to it and tried to catch it as usual. But this time a groom shouted at him and closed the poor chicken in the stables. White Fang was beside himself with anger. At that moment he bit the groom on his left hand, snarling loudly and dangerously. The man was very scared. He tried to run into the stables, but White Fang followed him too closely. Luckily, Collie ran up to White Fang and attacked him from behind suddenly. The groom ran into the stables and White Fang walked away again. But Collie did not leave him alone. She ran after him for a long time snarling as angrily as she could. In the end, White Fang had to run away from her across the fields.
