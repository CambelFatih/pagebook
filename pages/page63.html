Two nights later, White Fang made another mistake. He wanted to hunt his food and eat it joyfully. He went into the stables secretly and looked at the chickens. A moment later he began to kill the chickens one by one. In the morning, Weedon Scott entered the stables and saw fifty dead chickens on the floor. He called White Fang to come to him right away. Then he began to talk to his dog angrily. After that he put White Fang's nose down to the dead chickens, and at the same time hit him roughly on his head. The next morning, Judge Scott entered the stables and said, 'White Fang, you're smarter than I thought.' Thus White Fang learned to leave all living things alone in Sierra Vista. He obeyed his master in every situation. There were many butcher-shops where meat lay on the table. This meat he must not touch. There were cats in the master's house. He must leave them alone. He must eat the meat his master gave him, and nothing more.
