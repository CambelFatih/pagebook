Chapter 17

The Call of Kind

The months came and went. White Fang was very happy in the Southland. Human kindness shone on him like a sun. He grew like a flower in good soil. And yet he was still very different from other dogs. There was still some wildness in his eyes and the wolf in him was just asleep. He never played with other dogs. He lived separately as usual. Besides, all the dogs looked at him with fear. He gave them the fear of the wild, and they always met him with a snarl. White Fang did not attack these dogs. His white Fangs were enough to send them away once and for all. But there was only one problem in White Fang's life - Collie. She never left him in peace. She had never forgiven him for hurting the groom and killing the chickens. She was rude to him all the time. She became like poison to him. White Fang still ignored and avoided her every day. All was well with White Fang, except Collie. He had learned control and he knew the rules. Life was happy and easy. Neither fear nor danger came his way. One day, Weedon Scott rode a horse in the fields. White Fang always followed him during such journeys. He ran after the master's horse joyfully. He never stopped. His body did not feel tired at all. He could run in front of the horse for fifty miles easily. That day, something dangerous happened: a rabbit ran under the horse's feet suddenly and scared the horse very much. The horse turned left and right, pulled Weedon Scott off its back, and ran away across the field. As a result, Weedon Scott broke his leg very seriously. White Fang did not know what to do in that situation.
