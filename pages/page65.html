"Go home! Tell them what has happened to me! Go home!" cried Scott to White Fang. White Fang did not want to leave him alone. But the master again said, "White Fang, go home." White Fang had no right to do otherwise. White Fang knew the meaning of 'home' very well. So he turned and ran away. The family was in the kitchen when White Fang came. The children met White Fang with happy cries and ran up to him quickly. He ran away from them carefully and looked at his master's ife seriously. White Fang stood before her and began to snarl loudly. Then he took hold of her dress and pulled it strongly. Alice screamed with fear at that moment. "Go away, wolf!" cried Alice. White Fang stopped snarling loudly. He looked at their faces attentively. Then he began to bark without reason. "I think he is trying to tell us something," said Beth. "Something has happened to Weedon," said Alice. They were all scared at that moment. White Fang ran out of the kitchen, looking back for them to follow. After this accident the people were very kind to White Fang. The days came and went. One day, White Fang noticed a strange change - Collie was kind to him too. She did not attack him anymore. She even wanted to play with him sometimes. So White Fang was kind to her in return. One day, she led him through a long field into the forest. White Fang followed her without stopping. He ran with Collie as his mother Kitche and old One Eye had run one day in the silent Northland forest.
