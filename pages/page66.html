Chapter 18

The Sleeping Wolf

Just around that time the newspapers wrote of a strange escape made from San Quentin prison. He was a cruel man. He was a human animal, wild and terrible. His name was Jim Hall. In San Quentin prison he was the most dangerous prisoner. punishment had not broken his wild spirit. The authorities made his spirit even angrier than before. During his third term in prison, he met a very bad guard. The guard was very cruel to Jim Hall. The guard carried a bunch of keys and a gun. Jim Hall had only his long hands and his sharp teeth. One day, he jumped on him and closed his teeth around the guard's throat like a wild animal in the forest. After that Jim Hall began to live in a closed cell. He lived there for three years. He never left this cell. He saw neither sky nor sunshine. Day and night were a black silence. He was in an iron tomb, buried alive. He did not see any humans. He did not speak to anyone. When a guard gave him his food he snarled like a wild animal. He hated everything in his life. He did not make a sound for days and weeks. He ate his own soul in the black silence. He was simply a monster. And then, one night, he ran away. The guards said it was impossible. Nevertheless, the cell was empty and the body of a dead guard lay on the floor. Two other guards lay in the corridor. He had killed them with the guns of the first guard. The authorities promised a reward of gold for Jim Hall, dead or alive. Brave farmers tried to find him for many days. And one day they found him. They were shooting at him with all the guns they had. Unbelievably, Jim Hall was able to run away.
