Chapter 2

The Grey cub

The grey cub was different from his brothers and sisters. They inherited the red colour of their fur from the she-wolf, He alone inherited the grey colour from his father. He looked like a real wolf. He knew his sisters and brothers very well. He played with them clumsily and slowly. He learned his mother by touch and smell. Most of the first month of his life he slept for a long time. But after a few months, he began to learn about his new world with great curiosity. Actually, his world was very small. Its limits were the walls of the cave. Still, he saw the bright light of the world outside. He always tried to reach this light by crawling toward the entrance of the cave. And his brothers and sisters followed his lead. Unfortunately, their mother always pushed them back from the entrance. He was an angry little cub. It was normal because he was a predator. His mother and father ate only meat. Yet he could snarl much louder than his brothers and sisters. It was he that first bit another cub by the ear angrily. And it was he that caused the mother the biggest trouble in the cave. The grey cub's desire for light grew from day to day. He always wanted to crawl towards the cave's entrance. Unfortunately, his mother pushed him back every time. The cub did not know any other place except for the cave. So to him the entrance of the cave was a wall of light. The light attracted him as a candle attracts flies. The cub knew that it was the way out. Nevertheless, he himself did not know anything about it. He did not know the outside world at all. The grey cub knew his father, too.
