Like most animals of the wild, he faced hunger early on. His father stopped bringing meat, and his mother had no milk after some time. The cubs cried with hunger all day. Then the cubs slept. The life that was in them grew weaker every moment. Some of them never woke up again. One Eye was desperate. He did his best to find a prey. The she-wolf, too, left her lair and went out in search of meat. When the grey cub woke up he noticed that his only sister was dead. His sister no longer lifted her head nor moved cheerfully. He could not understand what had happened. Then there came a time when his father disappeared once and for all. The she-wolf knew why One Eye never came back. He had died in battle with a wild lynx. She saw his dead body near the lynx's lair up the stream. But she did not try to fight with the lynx that had many hungry kittens. motherhood is motherhood. besides, the lynx was much stronger and angrier during a fight. When his mother left the cave to hunt the grey cub never tried to leave the cave. It was a rule he would not break. So he just slept or waited for his mother. He did not leave the cave because of fear as well. He learnt it from One Eye and the she-wolf. So the grey cub knew fear, the heritage of the wild which no animal can escape. instinct needed law and control. But growth needed disobedience. That is why one day the grey cub crawled toward the entrance of the cave when his mother was not there. His fear was gone and only interest to live. The next moment he was in the outside world. It was strange. The light was very bright. Fear made him go back, but youth pushed him forward. He could not see anything for a while.
